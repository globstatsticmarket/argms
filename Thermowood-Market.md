<h2 data-start=""1340"" data-end=""1385""><strong data-start=""1343"" data-end=""1383"">The Thermowood Manufacturing Process</strong></h2>
<p data-start=""1387"" data-end=""1646"">Thermowood is produced through a <strong data-start=""1420"" data-end=""1446"">heat treatment process</strong> where timber is exposed to high temperatures (180&deg;C - 230&deg;C) in an <strong data-start=""1514"" data-end=""1541"">oxygen-free environment</strong>. This modification enhances the wood&rsquo;s durability, stability, and resistance to environmental factors.</p>
<h3 data-start=""1648"" data-end=""1690""><strong data-start=""1652"" data-end=""1688"">Steps in the Thermowood Process:</strong></h3>
<ol data-start=""1691"" data-end=""2037"">
<li data-start=""1691"" data-end=""1759""><strong data-start=""1694"" data-end=""1712"">Heating Phase:</strong> Wood is gradually heated to remove moisture.</li>
<li data-start=""1760"" data-end=""1848""><strong data-start=""1763"" data-end=""1788"">Thermal Modification:</strong> The temperature increases to modify the wood&rsquo;s structure.</li>
<li data-start=""1849"" data-end=""1944""><strong data-start=""1852"" data-end=""1881"">Cooling and Moisturizing:</strong> Wood is slowly cooled and rehydrated to prevent brittleness.</li>
<li data-start=""1945"" data-end=""2037""><strong data-start=""1948"" data-end=""1969"">Final Processing:</strong> The treated wood is cut, shaped, or processed for commercial use.</li>
</ol>
