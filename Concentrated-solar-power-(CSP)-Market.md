<h2 data-start=""1190"" data-end=""1238""><strong data-start=""1193"" data-end=""1236"">What is Concentrated Solar Power (CSP)?</strong></h2>
<p data-start=""1240"" data-end=""1519""><strong data-start=""1240"" data-end=""1274"">Concentrated Solar Power (CSP)</strong> is a renewable energy technology that uses <strong data-start=""1318"" data-end=""1339"">mirrors or lenses</strong> to concentrate sunlight onto a <strong data-start=""1371"" data-end=""1393"">single focal point</strong>, generating heat. This heat is used to produce <strong data-start=""1441"" data-end=""1450"">steam</strong>, which drives a <strong data-start=""1467"" data-end=""1516"">turbine connected to an electricity generator</strong>.</p>
<p data-start=""1521"" data-end=""1841"">CSP plants are designed for <strong data-start=""1549"" data-end=""1583"">utility-scale power generation</strong> and often include <strong data-start=""1602"" data-end=""1642"">thermal energy storage (TES) systems</strong>, allowing energy to be stored and used even when the sun isn&rsquo;t shining. This makes CSP a more <strong data-start=""1737"" data-end=""1749"">reliable</strong> and <strong data-start=""1754"" data-end=""1770"">dispatchable</strong> form of renewable energy compared to photovoltaic (PV) solar panels.</p>
<h2 data-start=""1848"" data-end=""1904""><strong data-start=""1851"" data-end=""1902"">Types of Concentrated Solar Power (CSP) Systems</strong></h2>
<h3 data-start=""1906"" data-end=""1943""><strong data-start=""1910"" data-end=""1941"">1. Parabolic Trough Systems</strong></h3>
<ul data-start=""1944"" data-end=""2177"">
<li data-start=""1944"" data-end=""2043"">Uses <strong data-start=""1951"" data-end=""1969"">curved mirrors</strong> to focus sunlight onto a receiver tube filled with heat transfer fluid.</li>
<li data-start=""2044"" data-end=""2115"">The heated fluid generates steam, which powers a <strong data-start=""2095"" data-end=""2112"">steam turbine</strong>.</li>
<li data-start=""2116"" data-end=""2177"">Most common CSP technology, used in large-scale projects.</li>
</ul>
<h3 data-start=""2179"" data-end=""2217""><strong data-start=""2183"" data-end=""2215"">2. Solar Power Tower Systems</strong></h3>
<ul data-start=""2218"" data-end=""2447"">
<li data-start=""2218"" data-end=""2296"">Uses <strong data-start=""2225"" data-end=""2254"">heliostats (flat mirrors)</strong> to focus sunlight onto a central tower.</li>
<li data-start=""2297"" data-end=""2381"">The tower contains <strong data-start=""2318"" data-end=""2333"">molten salt</strong>, which stores heat and generates electricity.</li>
<li data-start=""2382"" data-end=""2447"">Allows for <strong data-start=""2395"" data-end=""2420"">longer energy storage</strong> and improved efficiency.</li>
</ul>
<h3 data-start=""2449"" data-end=""2494""><strong data-start=""2453"" data-end=""2492"">3. Linear Fresnel Reflector Systems</strong></h3>
<ul data-start=""2495"" data-end=""2658"">
<li data-start=""2495"" data-end=""2582"">Uses <strong data-start=""2502"" data-end=""2524"">flat, long mirrors</strong> that track the sun and focus light on a fixed receiver.</li>
<li data-start=""2583"" data-end=""2658"">Less expensive than parabolic trough systems but with lower efficiency.</li>
</ul>
<h3 data-start=""2660"" data-end=""2692""><strong data-start=""2664"" data-end=""2690"">4. Dish/Engine Systems</strong></h3>
<ul data-start=""2693"" data-end=""2887"">
<li data-start=""2693"" data-end=""2767"">Uses <strong data-start=""2700"" data-end=""2726"">parabolic dish mirrors</strong> to direct sunlight onto a focal point.</li>
<li data-start=""2768"" data-end=""2833"">The heat drives a <strong data-start=""2788"" data-end=""2807"">Stirling engine</strong> to produce electricity.</li>
<li data-start=""2834"" data-end=""2887"">More suitable for <strong data-start=""2854"" data-end=""2884"">smaller-scale applications</strong>.</li>
</ul>
