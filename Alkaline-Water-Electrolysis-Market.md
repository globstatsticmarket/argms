<h2 data-start=""1119"" data-end=""1164""><strong data-start=""1122"" data-end=""1162"">What is Alkaline Water Electrolysis?</strong></h2>
<p data-start=""1166"" data-end=""1493"">Alkaline Water Electrolysis (AWE) is a well-established <strong data-start=""1222"" data-end=""1249"">electrochemical process</strong> used for the <strong data-start=""1263"" data-end=""1300"">production of hydrogen from water</strong>. It is considered one of the most mature and cost-effective technologies for <strong data-start=""1378"" data-end=""1407"">green hydrogen production</strong>, making it a crucial part of the global transition to <strong data-start=""1462"" data-end=""1490"">renewable energy sources</strong>.</p>
<p data-start=""1495"" data-end=""1797"">In AWE, an <strong data-start=""1506"" data-end=""1604"">alkaline electrolyte solution (typically potassium hydroxide - KOH or sodium hydroxide - NaOH)</strong> is used to facilitate the splitting of water molecules into <strong data-start=""1665"" data-end=""1688"">hydrogen and oxygen</strong>. This process takes place in an <strong data-start=""1721"" data-end=""1737"">electrolyzer</strong>, which consists of electrodes, separators, and membranes.</p>
<h2 data-start=""1804"" data-end=""1850""><strong data-start=""1807"" data-end=""1848"">How Alkaline Water Electrolysis Works</strong></h2>
<h3 data-start=""1852"" data-end=""1883""><strong data-start=""1856"" data-end=""1881"">Step-by-Step Process:</strong></h3>
<ol data-start=""1884"" data-end=""2611"">
<li data-start=""1884"" data-end=""2086"">
<p data-start=""1887"" data-end=""1915""><strong data-start=""1887"" data-end=""1913"">Electrolysis Reaction:</strong></p>
<ul data-start=""1919"" data-end=""2086"">
<li data-start=""1919"" data-end=""1971"">Water (H2O) is introduced into the electrolyzer.</li>
<li data-start=""1975"" data-end=""2086"">The alkaline electrolyte enables the <strong data-start=""2014"" data-end=""2060"">efficient movement of hydroxide ions (OH?)</strong> between the electrodes.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li data-start=""2088"" data-end=""2275"">
<p data-start=""2091"" data-end=""2128""><strong data-start=""2091"" data-end=""2126"">Hydrogen and Oxygen Generation:</strong></p>
<ul data-start=""2132"" data-end=""2275"">
<li data-start=""2132"" data-end=""2206"">At the <strong data-start=""2141"" data-end=""2152"">cathode</strong>, water is reduced, releasing <strong data-start=""2182"" data-end=""2203"">hydrogen gas (H2)</strong>.</li>
<li data-start=""2210"" data-end=""2275"">At the <strong data-start=""2219"" data-end=""2228"">anode</strong>, oxygen gas (O2) is released as a byproduct.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li data-start=""2277"" data-end=""2419"">
<p data-start=""2280"" data-end=""2301""><strong data-start=""2280"" data-end=""2299"">Gas Separation:</strong></p>
<ul data-start=""2305"" data-end=""2419"">
<li data-start=""2305"" data-end=""2419"">The hydrogen and oxygen gases are separated using <strong data-start=""2357"" data-end=""2391"">porous diaphragms or membranes</strong> to prevent recombination.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li data-start=""2421"" data-end=""2611"">
<p data-start=""2424"" data-end=""2462""><strong data-start=""2424"" data-end=""2460"">Hydrogen Collection and Storage:</strong></p>
<ul data-start=""2466"" data-end=""2611"">
<li data-start=""2466"" data-end=""2611"">The purified hydrogen gas is collected and stored for <strong data-start=""2522"" data-end=""2546"">various applications</strong>, including <strong data-start=""2558"" data-end=""2608"">fuel cells, industrial use, and energy storage</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>
