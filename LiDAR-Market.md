<h2 data-start=""878"" data-end=""915""><strong data-start=""881"" data-end=""915"">Understanding LiDAR Technology</strong></h2>
<p data-start=""917"" data-end=""1245""><strong data-start=""917"" data-end=""926"">LiDAR</strong> is a remote sensing method that uses laser light to measure distances to a target. By emitting laser pulses and measuring the time it takes for them to return after hitting an object, LiDAR systems can create precise, three-dimensional information about the shape and surface characteristics of objects and landscapes.</p>
<h3 data-start=""1247"" data-end=""1287""><strong data-start=""1251"" data-end=""1287"">Key Components of LiDAR Systems:</strong></h3>
<ol data-start=""1289"" data-end=""1694"">
<li data-start=""1289"" data-end=""1347""><strong data-start=""1292"" data-end=""1309"">Laser Source:</strong> Emits laser pulses toward the target.</li>
<li data-start=""1348"" data-end=""1433""><strong data-start=""1351"" data-end=""1374"">Scanner and Optics:</strong> Directs the laser beam and collects the reflected signals.</li>
<li data-start=""1434"" data-end=""1538""><strong data-start=""1437"" data-end=""1480"">Photodetector and Receiver Electronics:</strong> Detects the returned signals and converts them into data.</li>
<li data-start=""1539"" data-end=""1694""><strong data-start=""1542"" data-end=""1581"">Positioning and Navigation Systems:</strong> Utilize GPS and inertial measurement units (IMUs) to determine the exact location and orientation of the sensor.</li>
</ol>
