<h3 data-start=""1122"" data-end=""1157""><strong data-start=""1126"" data-end=""1155"">What Is Industrial Sugar?</strong></h3>
<p data-start=""1158"" data-end=""1412"">Industrial sugar refers to <strong data-start=""1185"" data-end=""1253"">sugar used in large-scale commercial and industrial applications</strong>, primarily in <strong data-start=""1268"" data-end=""1354"">food processing, beverages, confectionery, pharmaceuticals, and ethanol production</strong>. <li data-start=""1565"" data-end=""1696""><strong data-start=""1568"" data-end=""1583"">Brown Sugar</strong> &ndash; Contains <strong data-start=""1595"" data-end=""1607"">molasses</strong>, giving it a distinct color and flavor, used in <strong data-start=""1656"" data-end=""1693"">bakery and confectionery products</strong>.</li>
<li data-start=""1697"" data-end=""1795""><strong data-start=""1700"" data-end=""1724"">Liquid Sugar (Syrup)</strong> &ndash; Used in <strong data-start=""1735"" data-end=""1792"">soft drinks, energy drinks, and pharmaceutical syrups</strong>.</li>
<li data-start=""1796"" data-end=""1903""><strong data-start=""1799"" data-end=""1815"">Invert Sugar</strong> &ndash; A mixture of <strong data-start=""1831"" data-end=""1855"">glucose and fructose</strong>, used in <strong data-start=""1865"" data-end=""1900"">ice creams, beverages, and jams</strong>.</li>
<li data-start=""1904"" data-end=""2023""><strong data-start=""1907"" data-end=""1920"">Raw Sugar</strong> &ndash; Less processed than white sugar, often used in <strong data-start=""1970"" data-end=""2020"">industrial fermentation and ethanol production</strong>.</li>
</ol>
